        <section>
            <h2>What is a Tornado?</h2>
            <p>A tornado is a rapidly rotating column of air that is in contact with both the surface of the Earth and a cumulonimbus cloud or, in rare cases, the base of a cumulus cloud.</p>
        </section>
        <section>
            <h2>Formation</h2>
            <p>Tornadoes typically form from severe thunderstorms when warm, moist air meets cold, dry air, creating instability in the atmosphere.</p>
        </section>
        <section>
            <h2>Characteristics</h2>
            <ul>
                <li>Tornadoes can vary in size and intensity.</li>
                <li>They are often accompanied by strong winds, hail, and thunderstorms.</li>
                <li>The Enhanced Fujita (EF) Scale is used to categorize tornadoes based on their intensity and the damage they cause.</li>
            </ul>
        </section>
        <section>
            <h2>Safety Tips</h2>
            <ul>
                <li>Seek shelter in a sturdy building or storm shelter.</li>
                <li>Avoid windows and stay away from doors.</li>
                <li>If caught outdoors, seek low-lying areas and lie flat, covering your head.</li>
            </ul>
